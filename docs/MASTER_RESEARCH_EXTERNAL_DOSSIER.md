## 2. Which Strategies Have REAL Documented Edge — and Why, With Parameters

Patterns and indicators in isolation are folklore; the edge lives in **conjunctions** — trend alignment + a clean trigger + volume confirmation + regime filter + disciplined trailing stop. Below, ranked by reproducibility and net-of-cost evidence, are the families worth this system's capital. Most map onto modules already in the codebase.

### Tier 1 — Strongest published evidence (academic, multi-year, net-of-cost)

**ORB "Stocks-in-Play" (5-min) — the single highest-leverage add.** Zarattini, Barbon & Aziz (2024), 7,000+ US stocks 2016–2023. Mark the 5-min OR high/low at 9:35 ET, enter on first close beyond it, stop on the opposite side, flatten EOD. The decisive filter is **Stocks-in-Play (SIP)** — names with news-driven abnormal volume that day. Top-20 SIP portfolio: **1,600%+ net return, 36% annualized alpha, Sharpe 2.81**; 5-min beat 15/30/60-min on both return and drawdown. *Without the SIP filter, results degrade sharply* — the authors are explicit the filter, not the ORB mechanic, is the edge. Implementation: pre-market RVOL ≥ 3× 20-day avg + news flag; 500k+ ADV; $10–$50 price band; stop = OR-low; target trail or 1.5–2× OR range; most of the move is done in 60–90 min (https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4729284).

**VWAP Trend-Follow on QQQ/TQQQ.** Zarattini & Aziz (2023), Jan 2018–Sep 2023 (two bear markets). Long while price is above intraday VWAP, flat/short below; flatten EOD. QQQ: **671% net, max DD 9.4%, Sharpe 2.1**; TQQQ: 8,242%. The edge is *continuation* while price holds the VWAP side, not reversion to it. Maróy's 2024 follow-up adds "ladder" exits to push Sharpe above 3.0 on SPY. Directly applicable to already-promoted SPY/QQQ/XLK/SMH/TQQQ. *Size TQQQ one Kelly fraction below QQQ* — 3× leverage carries path-dependent decay (https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4631351).

**Intraday Noise-Boundary Momentum on SPY.** Zarattini, Aziz & Barbon (2024), 2007–2024. Each day compute a "noise boundary" = open ± the 14-day average open-to-now move; at each HH:00/HH:30 check, if price is outside the band, open a trend position in that direction; exit when price crosses back through intraday VWAP (VWAP-as-trailing-stop); flatten EOD. **19.6% CAGR, Sharpe 1.33, ~43% win rate** — convex, big-winner/small-loser profile, beta near zero. An independent reviewer flagged undocumented execution assumptions and no disclosed OOS test, so treat the Sharpe as an upper bound. The VWAP-as-stop rule is already consistent with this system's trailing architecture (https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4824172, reviewer: https://quantmacro.substack.com/p/paper-review-an-effective-intraday).

**RSI-2 EOD Mean Reversion (the system's existing edge, formalized).** Connors RSI-2 on QQQ: price > 200-day SMA, buy when RSI(2) closes < 5, exit when RSI(2) closes > 65. **CAGR 12.7% vs 9% buy-and-hold, Sharpe 2.85, 75% win rate, profit factor 3.0, max DD 19.5%.** Adding a 50-SMA filter (price > both 200 and 50 SMA) lifts win rate 5–10% at lower frequency. A clean, standalone EOD swing signal for already-promoted SPY/QQQ/XLK/SMH/DIA/XLY/XLC. Weak spot: persistent downtrends (2022, 2008) — the 200-SMA gate catches most of it (https://www.mql5.com/en/articles/17636).

### Tier 2 — Solid supporting evidence

**Overnight / Close-to-Open hold.** Overnight captures a disproportionate share of long-run index returns; one model holding QQQ into the close 4 nights/week captures 92–95% of QQQ's 26-year gains. Edge concentrates in **large-cap ETFs (SPY/QQQ), not small-caps** — do not apply buy-the-close logic to individual small/mid-cap names. Overnight sizing must bake in gap risk: size on `ATR × gap-multiplier (~1.5)`, not the intraday ATR stop alone (April 2025 saw QQQ gap −3.2%) (https://www.shareplanner.com/blog/strategies-for-trading/fading-the-gap-how-large-overnight-moves-in-spy-and-qqq-play-out-during-the-trading-day.html).

**Gap-and-Go with ORB confirmation** (the retail-friendly cousin of SIP). 190,000+ trades / 600+ symbols: 5/15/30-min ORB win rates 52.2–52.9% — the edge is asymmetric R:R (≥2:1), not win rate. Classify gaps: trade breakaway/continuation (catalyst + RVOL ≥ 2×), fade or skip common/exhaustion gaps. Best combined with the SIP filter (https://orbsetups.com/research/gap-and-go-trading-strategy-how-to-combine-pre-market-gaps-with-opening-range-breakouts/).

### Tier 3 — Use as filters/management layers, not primary signals

**Breakout-Retest** as a *second-chance entry*: if an ORB/gap fires but the system misses the initial thrust, re-enter on a confirming candle when price retests the broken level (within ~0.1 ATR) with a tighter stop. Practitioner win rate 55–65% on confirmed retests vs 40–50% on immediate breakouts; no clean academic OOS Sharpe (https://medium.com/@FMZQuant/advanced-multi-timeframe-breakout-retest-trading-strategy-eb1e669b6ed4).

**Donchian / ATR trailing trend-following (already running)** — keep as the **swing exit/position-management layer, not a new intraday-entry signal**. Equities are the hardest asset class for pure trend-following (win rates 30–40%, single-index Sharpe 0.5–0.7); ATR trailing adds ~0.2–0.25 Sharpe over a simple exit. Its value: regime diversification (it makes money in persistent trends that kill MR) and it lets trend-day ORB entries ride into multi-day moves on a ratcheting stop (https://quantpedia.com/designing-robust-trend-following-system/).

### Candlestick & chart patterns — the honest construction

Standalone patterns are at-best marginal: Marshall/Young/Rose (2006) found no edge on the Dow 30; on FTSE 1-min bars *zero* patterns cleared 1% net of spread; Bulkowski's 4.7M-candle database shows the "bullish" inverted hammer continues *bearish* 65% of the time (https://medium.com/@trading.dude/the-truth-about-candlestick-patterns-are-they-still-profitable-or-just-trading-folklore-6bd1080581cd). **But with a filter stack applied, the same patterns become tradeable** (TradesViz backtests: Three White Soldiers + RSI = 83% WR / 2.68 PF; Bearish-Engulfing-as-MR-long = 76% WR / 2.73 PF — though on 6-month, 33–78-trade windows, so hypothesis-grade not deployment-grade) (https://www.tradesviz.com/blog/candlestick-pattern-effectiveness-backtesting/).

The three pattern types with the best evidence for this universe:
- **NR7 / NR7-Inside-Day** (Crabel) — volatility-contraction-precedes-expansion. Enter above NR7ID high at next open + "stretch" (10-day avg of smaller open-to-extreme); stop below NR7ID low. *Complementary to the existing Donchian:* require an NR7/NR4 condition in the 1–3 days before a Donchian breakout to raise conviction and cut false breaks — the exact failure mode that hit the system in early June (https://tradingstrategiesdaily.com/p/nr7id-toby-crabel).
- **Flags/pennants** — strongest continuation evidence, 65–80% WR when formed after a sharp pole and entered on *confirmed* breakout (80% on AAPL, 50% on BTC — works best in trending large-cap equities, which is this universe). Use 65% as the planning estimate (https://www.quantifiedstrategies.com/pennant-trading-strategy/).
- **Hammer at 21-EMA support in an uptrend** — body < 25% of range, lower shadow ≥ 2× body; enter next open, trail at 2.5× ATR(14).

**The four-layer eligibility gate that converts a pattern into edge** (the conjunction *is* the alpha): (1) **Regime** — stock > 200-day MA and SPY > 50-day MA; (2) **Trend** — price > 21-EMA > 50-EMA; (3) **Relative Strength** — IBD-style RS > 80 vs universe; (4) **Volume** — signal bar ≥ 1.5× 10-session avg. All four required; adding volume confirmation alone moved one ES backtest from coin-flip to 75.76% WR (https://chartalert.in/2023/12/04/relative-strength-rating-relative-strength-line/).

**Decay/crowding caveat across all of Section 2:** ORB and VWAP are among the most widely known retail approaches; the SIP filter and noise-boundary overlay are the published *differentiators*, and vanilla versions have compressed since the 2020 retail surge. All Zarattini backtests cover a structural bull (2016–2024) with no documented post-publication OOS; assume 20–30% slippage drag and treat the Sharpes as upper bounds. Best edge environment for all of these is moderate VIX (15–30) — hence the regime gate below is not optional.

## 3. How AI Can Genuinely Contribute to This System

The honest 2026 picture: LLMs/ML have moved from hype to **narrow, demonstrable edge in supporting roles** (regime filtering, event parsing, automated factor research) and **fail badly as autonomous traders or direct price predictors**. The evidence points squarely at AI as a *research-and-filtering amplifier*, not a signal generator — which is exactly the layer this system (with its existing `llm_codegen.py`, `llm_strategy_generator.py`, and `walk_forward.py`) is built to host.

**What does NOT work — stop here, save the engineering:**
- **Autonomous LLM trading agents.** The 77-study Agentic Trading survey found zero studies hit reproducibility tier R3, only 1 reported transaction costs. StockBench (real OOS, Mar–Jul 2025) had top performer Kimi-K2 at 1.9% vs 0.4% buy-and-hold — essentially no alpha, and *all* agents underperformed the passive baseline in bearish conditions. LLMs are structurally bullish, lack hedging logic, and degrade past ~20-stock portfolios (https://arxiv.org/html/2605.19337v1, https://arxiv.org/html/2510.02209v1).
- **Reinforcement learning for directional retail trading.** A 167-study meta-analysis found *no* significant difference between Policy Gradient and DQN (p=0.640); the only real edge was in market-making, which needs tick-level infrastructure a solo operator lacks. Not worth the cost (https://arxiv.org/html/2512.10913v1).
- **Sentiment as a primary signal.** Real but thin and fast-decaying edge (a 2025 S&P 500 study hit 5.77% vs −0.70% buy-and-hold — but with *zero* assumed transaction costs and a 4-month window). LLM sentiment strategies show ~30% alpha decay within 6 months because everyone runs the same models on the same feeds. Use only as a confirming filter, never the entry trigger, and rotate sources (headlines → 8-Ks) to slow crowding (https://arxiv.org/html/2507.09739v1, https://arxiv.org/html/2512.11913v1).

**What genuinely works — the three integrations to build, in priority order:**

1. **Regime gate (VIX + ADX) — implement first.** This is the cleanest documented edge for a solo operator. A VIX 200-day-MA regime gate backtested 2005–2025 cut max drawdown from −55% to −22% while preserving returns (9.2% vs 9.8% annualized), lifting Sharpe 0.45 → 0.72. ML-based detection cuts identification lag from 3–5 days to 0–2 (the difference between a 2.5% and a 12.5% daily loss in March 2020). RegimeFolio reports up to 20% lower forecast error and >0.5 Sharpe improvement (https://volatilitybox.com/research/volatility-regime-detection/, https://arxiv.org/html/2510.14986v1). **No ML required for v1** — the rules-based VIX-200d-MA + ADX version has the best evidence and lowest decay. Write a daily pre-market regime score (risk-on / transitional / risk-off) to a config/DB field that intraday and swing sizing both read. Caveat: ~40% false-positive rate without multi-signal consensus, and it works best on SPY/large-cap, less so on individual small-caps or intraday.

2. **Earnings/event quarantine filter.** Pure risk management, zero prediction, zero crowding risk (the calendar is public). Have Claude parse a daily catalyst calendar (the existing `earnings_calendar.py` / `macro_fetcher.py` modules are the hooks) and flag symbols with same-day/next-day earnings, plus FOMC/CPI dates; auto-reduce sizing to 25% or skip. This directly addresses the system's documented event-volatility vulnerability (https://arxiv.org/html/2605.19337v1).

3. **Overnight Claude research loop (QuantaAlpha-style).** The strongest case for AI in this system. The QuantaAlpha practitioner ran a Claude Code evolutionary loop generating 20 factors against 53 commodity futures — **80% (16/20) showed positive OOS predictive power, best factor 1.72 Sharpe / 38.7% annualized OOS.** The keys were a *constrained DSL* (not arbitrary Python), strict train/test separation, and crossover of top signals. QuantEvolve (1.52 Sharpe / 256% cumulative, equities Aug-2022–Jul-2025) and the LLM-alpha paper (53% OOS on SSE50 vs −13% benchmark) point the same direction (https://saulius.io/blog/claude-code-quant-research, https://arxiv.org/html/2510.18569v1, https://arxiv.org/html/2409.06289v3). Build: define a constrained strategy DSL (e.g., `entry = crossover(ema9, ema21) filtered by regime_flag, exit = trailing_stop(atr_multiple)`), have Claude generate 10–15 candidates/week, auto-backtest each against the system's own fills + price history, surface a ranked shortlist for review.

**The non-negotiable methodological guard:** ML *amplifies* overfitting, survivorship bias, and data leakage rather than solving them (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.00080). Any strategy the research loop proposes must show **positive OOS information coefficient across ≥3 non-overlapping 90-day holdout windows** before promotion to paper — this single gate, wired into the existing `walk_forward.py`, is the most important protection against ML-amplified curve-fitting. QuantaAlpha's own failure mode was instrument concentration (36% of returns from one Natural Gas signal); enforce diversification on any promoted set. *Caveats:* most positive ML results omit realistic costs; the 1.72 Sharpe is on commodity futures, not equities; LLM-strategy-gen papers use short windows and small universes. Track rolling 30-day Sharpe of every AI-surfaced signal separately and expect decay.

## 4. The Risk & Position-Sizing Framework That Makes Trailing-Stop Trend Systems Profitable

This is the part the practitioner literature is unanimous on: **risk management and position sizing are the primary edge; signal quality is secondary.** A mediocre signal with disciplined sizing beats a strong signal with poor sizing, because uncapped sizing lets a few large losers erase many small winners. The math below is what turns a 30–46% win-rate trend system into a positive-expectancy compounder — and almost all of it is a change to the sizing module, which currently uses a fixed `max_position_usd` cap rather than volatility-based sizing.

**Why sub-50% win rate is fine — expectancy is driven by payoff ratio, not frequency.** `E = (Win% × AvgWin) − (Loss% × AvgLoss)`. A 40% win rate at 2.5R winners / 1R losers yields E = (0.40 × 2.5R) − (0.60 × 1R) = **+0.4R per trade**. The 25-year TradingWithRayner multi-asset backtest delivered 14.5% annualized at 45.8% WR / 1.74 payoff (E = +0.255R), compounding to 2,866%. The fat right tail (5R/10R/20R runners) funds the system; losers are cut to exactly 1R. "Cut losses, let winners run" is the literal mechanism, not a platitude (https://www.tradingwithrayner.com/trend-following-strategy/).

**Trailing stop — ATR Chandelier is the evidence-backed default.** `LongStop = HighestHigh(N) − ATR(N) × M`. Multiplier backtest: 2× → PF 1.44 / DD 16%; 2.5× → 1.56 / 19%; **3× → 1.61 / 22%** (the daily sweet spot); 4× → 1.54 / 28%. The 3× setting beats a fixed-5% trail by 48% and fixed-10% by 26% (https://stratbase.ai/en/blog/average-true-range-trailing-stop). Method selection: **ATR/Chandelier as default** (adapts to volatility, works across regimes); Parabolic SAR only on mature trends *with a trend filter* (it bleeds capital in chop); never plain percent-based; structure/swing-low for *initial* stop placement. The hybrid best practice: **initial stop at swing-low (clean invalidation), switch to Chandelier trail once the trade is +1R** (https://www.aurra.markets/academy/advanced-guides/the-4-best-trailing-stop-strategies-to-maximize-profit).

**Position sizing — volatility targeting is what this system should run.** `Shares = (Equity × RiskPct) / (ATR(14) × Multiplier)`. This naturally shrinks size on volatile names and grows it on calm ones. Worked example: $100k account, 0.75% risk, stop = 1× ATR = $1.50 → $750 / $1.50 = **500 shares**. This replaces the current fixed-dollar/fixed-share cap (https://medium.com/@ildiveliu/risk-before-returns-position-sizing-frameworks-fixed-fractional-atr-based-kelly-lite-4513f770a82a).

**Kelly — use as a ceiling, never a default.** `K = (Win% × Payoff − Loss%) / Payoff`. At 40% WR / 2.5 payoff, full Kelly = 16% of equity — and full Kelly produces 50–60% drawdowns in practice. Half-Kelly retains ~75% of the growth rate at roughly half the variance; quarter-to-half Kelly is the professional band. The fixed-fractional 0.75% is the conservative safe anchor; Kelly is the ceiling you never exceed. **Critical:** Kelly inputs (WR, payoff) must come from *out-of-sample* data or the fraction is systematically oversized (https://coriva.eu.org/en/kelly-criterion-position-sizing/).

**Risk of ruin — the survivability math that sets per-trade risk.** Balsara: `RoR = ((1 − Edge)/(1 + Edge))^U`, where `U = Account / RiskPerTrade`. At Edge = 0.40: **1% risk/trade → RoR ≈ 0; 5% → ≈ 0.027%; 10% → ≈ 1.7% (unacceptable).** Cutting risk from 2% to 1% drops ruin probability 80–90% due to the exponential. Target RoR < 0.5% → **≤ 1% per trade** at the current edge estimate (https://www.backtestbase.com/education/risk-of-ruin-calculator-trading).

**Portfolio heat — the cap that survives a correlated selloff.** Heat = Σ(stop distance × position size) / equity. Cap at **6–8%**. Eight positions at 0.75% each = 6% heat = survivable. The correlation trap: tech/semi names are 80–90% correlated in a selloff — treat a cluster as one sizing unit (3 correlated names → 0.25% each so the cluster = 0.75%). The Balsara formula *underestimates* ruin in correlated environments (April 2025 tariff shock stopped many positions simultaneously); the heat cap and sector-correlation check are the practical mitigants, not formula tweaks (https://blog.traderspost.io/article/trend-following-strategies-guide).

**Drawdown recovery math (why DD < 25% is the target):** 10% DD needs +11.1%; 20% → +25%; 30% → +42.9%; 40% → +66.7%; 50% → +100%. A concentrated US-equity system (higher correlation than the diversified futures backtests) should target DD < 25% via ≤ 6% heat and ≤ 1% per-trade risk (https://journalplus.co/tools/max-drawdown-calculator/).

**Pyramiding — the fat-tail capture, but Phase N+1 only.** Concretum's 40-year backtest: vol-targeting alone = 11.46% IRR / 25.6% DD; vol-parity + pyramiding = 20.0% IRR / **48.7% DD** (average trade P&L doubles, but DD nearly doubles too). Rules: add only at +1R; each add = 0.5× the previous unit (1.0 / 0.5 / 0.25 / 0.125); move the initial stop to breakeven on each add; same Chandelier trail across the full position; cap at 3 adds. **Do not enable until 200+ completed live trades with logged R-multiples confirm the fat right tail is actually present** (https://concretumgroup.com/position-sizing-in-trend-following-comparing-volatility-targeting-volatility-parity-and-pyramiding/).

**The concrete parameter set this system should adopt:**
| Lever | Daily / swing | Intraday (5–15m) |
|---|---|---|
| Initial stop | 1.0× ATR(14) below nearest swing-low | same logic, tighter ATR |

## 5. How Successful Indie/Retail Systems Are Actually Built — and the Failure Modes to Avoid

The profitable retail minority share a consistent shape: **risk/sizing as the primary edge, signal as secondary, multi-layer validation before any live capital, and execution infrastructure that mirrors live conditions from day one.** Realistic targets cluster at **15–30% CAGR, max DD < 20%, Sharpe > 1.0** — and anything consistently *above* that range in a backtest should be treated as an overfitting red flag, not a trophy (Sharpe > 2.0 → suspect; 200%+ annual → almost certainly curve-fit) (https://www.quantconnect.com/forum/discussion/19451/, https://www.buildalpha.com/robustness-testing-guide/).

**The stack is already correct.** Alpaca (free paper+live, fractional, Python-first) is the dominant solo choice; QuantConnect's LEAN engine layers institutional-grade backtesting with realistic fills and direct Alpaca live integration. IBKR adds complexity only worth it for instrument coverage Alpaca lacks. This system is already on the recommended path (https://medium.com/@pta.forwork/comparing-algorithmic-trading-platforms-metatrader-interactive-brokers-ibkr-alpaca-3fc0fab11288).

**The paper-to-live gap is structural and quantifiable.** Alpaca's own docs: paper fills at last-traded price with no slippage/spread, unchecked liquidity, partial fills simulated at only 10% random frequency. A documented case showed 12% paper profit → 5% live loss after slippage and fees. 67.2% of Alpaca API users went live with no paper trading at all. Fix: **model slippage explicitly** — 5–15 bps/fill for mid-cap equities, 20–50 bps for small/thin names — and any intraday strategy must show positive expectancy *after* costs on OOS data, never gross (https://alpaca.markets/learn/paper-trading-vs-live-trading-a-data-backed-guide-on-when-to-start-trading-real-money). For intraday this is the killing field: a 0.15%/trade gross edge firing 50×/day pays 5–10 bps round-trip and nets ~nothing (https://rmoneyindia.com/support/algo-trading-mistakes-loss-reasons-2025/).

**The five documented failure modes — and the fixes:**
1. **Overfitting.** Detect via parameter sensitivity (if RSI-14 works but 13/15 collapse, it's overfit). Fix: ≤ 5–7 core parameters per strategy; 30% data as a true never-seen holdout; walk-forward over rolling windows; cross-market sanity check (an SPY signal should *degrade gracefully* on QQQ/DIA, not collapse); Monte-Carlo resample of trade sequences. The `walk_forward.py` already in the repo is the hook (https://www.buildalpha.com/robustness-testing-guide/).
2. **Transaction costs / over-trading.** The most active retail traders earned 11.4% net vs 17.9% market — the 6.5-point gap was almost entirely excess trading. Embed realistic costs before claiming edge.
3. **Fragile execution.** ~22% of retail live losses attributed to infrastructure (connectivity, stale feeds, mis-routing). The **sub-penny stop bug this system already hit** (4dp vs 2dp tick → all hard stops rejected by Alpaca) is the canonical example. Fixes: tick-size validation before every submit; reconcile local state vs broker every loop; reconnection logic; treat every order rejection as a logged/alerted system event (https://www.utradealgos.com/blog/what-every-trader-should-know-about-algorithmic-trading-risks).
4. **Regime mismatch.** The SG Trend Index posted its 2nd-largest drawdown since 2000 (−20.4%, May-2024→May-2025) precisely in the choppy/reversal regime — the same condition that hit this system in early June. Fix: each strategy needs its *own* regime filter (no pure trend-follower without an ADX > 20 gate; no pure MR without a volatility floor). Running trend + MR together with low drawdown correlation (−0.13 in the 2006–2025 backtest) smooths the equity curve more than either alone (https://www.algomatictrading.com/post/why-two-strategies-are-better-than-one-trend-following-mean-reversion).
5. **Emotional override.** The "set-and-forget then panic-disable after a losing stretch, miss the recovery" cycle. Fix: rule-based pauses only (max daily DD 3%, strategy DD 15%), never manual feeling-based intervention; log all manual overrides and review monthly — > 2× normal rate is itself a signal to re-evaluate (https://rmoneyindia.com/support/algo-trading-mistakes-loss-reasons-2025/).

**The operational checklist (synthesized from BuildAlpha / QuantStart / Alpaca / r/algotrading):** 30% holdout + walk-forward + cross-market + Monte-Carlo before live capital → realistic cost modeling (reject if net E < 0) → execution hardening (tick validation, per-loop reconciliation, 3%-daily-DD kill switch) → sizing discipline (start at **0.25% risk/trade for the first 50 live fills**, scale only after live slippage matches backtest) → live monitoring (flag > 3% daily DD, quarterly recalibration, log all rejections, weekly fill-vs-expected comparison) → regime awareness → correlation management (target inter-strategy drawdown correlation < 0.3) (https://www.quantstart.com/articles/Can-Algorithmic-Traders-Still-Succeed-at-the-Retail-Level/).

**What this system is already doing right** (and the encouraging part): the **Donchian-only reset is textbook** — reduce to one proven strategy, confirm execution reliability, then re-add strategies one at a time, each with an independent regime filter and only if its live drawdown correlation with Donchian is < 0.3. The sub-penny stop fix, the ATR-based-sizing direction, the walk-forward script, and the existing LLM-codegen pipeline all map directly onto the proven path. The single most important next verification step is confirming the **sub-penny hard stops are actually resting on the book in live runs** before any strategy is re-added.

*Caveats:* no audited live-P&L distribution exists for retail algos — all CAGR figures are calibration anchors, not guarantees. The −20.4% SG Trend figure is institutional CTA, not retail equity. Trend-following's 2024–25 struggle appears regime-specific (chop) rather than structural crowding (Cambridge Associates finds no systemic crowding-out at retail scale).
